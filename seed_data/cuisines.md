# Indian Cuisines Reference

## Biryani
Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating from the Indian subcontinent, made with Indian spices, rice, and meat (chicken, mutton, beef) or vegetables. Hyderabadi biryani uses the dum cooking method. Lucknowi biryani is milder. Kolkata biryani includes potato and egg. Typical price range in India: ₹250-₹600 per plate at mid-range restaurants. Common pairings: raita, salan, boiled egg.

## North Indian
North Indian cuisine features dishes like butter chicken, paneer tikka, dal makhani, naan, and tandoori items. Heavy use of dairy (cream, butter, paneer) and tandoor cooking. Generally richer and more gravy-based than South Indian food. Popular vegetarian options: paneer butter masala, dal tadka, chole bhature.

## South Indian
South Indian cuisine emphasizes rice, lentils, and fermented foods. Iconic dishes: dosa (crispy crepe), idli (steamed cake), sambar (lentil stew), vada (savory donut), uttapam. Coconut, curry leaves, mustard seeds, and tamarind define the flavor. Naturally vegetarian-friendly. Typical breakfast price: ₹80-₹200.

## Chinese (Indian-Chinese)
Indo-Chinese cuisine is a fusion: Hakka noodles, gobi manchurian, chilli paneer, schezwan fried rice, hot and sour soup. Spicier and more sauce-heavy than authentic Chinese food. Found at most casual dining spots in India. Price range: ₹200-₹450.

## Vegetarian Indicators
"Pure veg" restaurants in India serve no meat, eggs, or sometimes onion/garlic (Jain food). Look for "Pure Veg" or green dot symbol on menus. Common veg proteins: paneer, soya, chickpeas, lentils, mushrooms.

## Budget Tiers (India, 2024)
- Street food / quick bite: under ₹150
- Casual dining: ₹150-₹400 per person
- Mid-range: ₹400-₹800 per person
- Upscale: ₹800+ per person

## Dietary Notes
- "Jain" = no onion, garlic, root vegetables
- "Sattvic" = pure vegetarian + no onion/garlic
- "Halal" = meat slaughtered per Islamic law
- "Eggetarian" = vegetarian + eggs
